Context
  • Several digital products already exist.
  • Different teams created inconsistent components.
  • Complaints and legal pressure are increasing.
  • The budget is limited and a full rebuild is impossible.
Make the transition explicit. Task 4 is where accessibility becomes a cross-system quality and governance decision.
Strategic lens

The best starting point is risk-based and component-based.

Do not start everywhere at once. Start where user impact, business criticality, and legal exposure are highest.

Fix first Critical journeys

Application forms, login, registration, service requests, uploads, and customer-portal navigation.

Stabilize next Shared components

Buttons, form fields, error messages, dialogs, navigation, tables, and upload elements.

High user relevance Visible drop-off risk Potential legal importance Reusable fixes
Push learners toward the idea that not all accessibility work has equal urgency. The risk-based and component-based combination is the central strategic move.
Governance

Accessibility only scales when responsibilities are clear.

Product Prioritizes journeys

Chooses the most critical user flows and business impact areas.

Design Defines patterns

Keeps visual consistency and accessible interaction logic aligned.

Frontend Builds components

Creates and maintains reusable accessible UI components.

QA + Legal Checks and accepts

Validates labels, keyboard access, contrast, structure, error handling, and risk areas.

Acceptance criteria
  • Keyboard access is testable and visible.
  • Labels and errors are clear in forms.
  • Shared components have approved patterns.
  • Accessibility review becomes part of delivery, not a late add-on.
The framework of responsibility matters here. Learners should see that accessible development needs ownership, review, and acceptance criteria.
Roadmap

A realistic twelve-month roadmap is more valuable than a perfect but impossible plan.

0 to 3 months Audit and urgent fixes

Audit critical journeys and repair the strongest barriers in forms, login, navigation, and error handling.

4 to 8 months Component system and review flow

Build a reusable accessible component library and introduce a lightweight review workflow.

9 to 12 months Scale and institutionalize

Expand across systems, add regular checks, and review remaining risks and next priorities.

Reinforce realism. A good plan sequences change instead of pretending all systems can be repaired at once.
Assignment

Develop the decision architecture for the next twelve months.

Work points 1. Define the strategic target picture for accessible web components. 2. Prioritize affected systems and user journeys. 3. Evaluate the most critical components. 4. Create a risk analysis with at least five risks. 5. Decide what should happen immediately, mid-term, and long-term. 6. Propose governance, review methods, responsibilities, and acceptance criteria. 7. Make one deliberate decision despite incomplete information. 8. Present a management recommendation and a twelve-month roadmap.
Core decision
Most important sentence Do not treat accessibility as a one-time correction project.

Treat it as a permanent quality and governance system that reduces risk, improves access, and increases long-term maintainability.

Close the deck by repeating the central management decision. That is the real transfer outcome of Day 4.
